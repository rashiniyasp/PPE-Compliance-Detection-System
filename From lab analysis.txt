TIP

What this means: Your model is actually highly successful at detecting people and the PPE they are wearing (all >80% mAP50). The overall score is being dragged down by the "negative classes" (e.g., no_helmet, no_boots).

Why are negative classes performing poorly?

Severe Class Imbalance: You have 151 examples of boots but only 4 examples of no_boots. The model simply didn't have enough data to learn what the absence of boots looks like.
Conceptual Difficulty: It is inherently harder for an object detection model to draw a bounding box around "nothing" (the absence of a helmet) than a physical object (a helmet).
IMPORTANT

Our architecture saves the day: This confirms that our design in pipeline.py is the correct approach. Instead of relying on the model to predict no_helmet, our pipeline detects the Person and the helmet. If a helmet is not detected and associated with the person's keypoints, our logic flags it as a violation. We can completely ignore the poor performance on the negative classes.
